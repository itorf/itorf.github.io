About Me

I took my first course building websites in college, and I've been hooked ever since. Although my pursuit of a social science degree was well underway, I realized that there had to be some value in combining an interest in people, and an interest in the Internet and web applications. I wanted to use my degrees in Psychology and Communication and combine them with my technical abilities to create websites that people would use and love.

After I graduated, I worked as a Technical Recruiter which gave me insight into the current tech industry, as well as which technologies were hot. While working full-time, I took extension courses in PHP and Javascript and supplemented them with other online resources. Pretty soon, I realized I needed to dive headfirst into this, and devote myself to it full-time. I applied and was accepted to App Academy, a 12-week coding bootcamp in San Francisco that teaches Ruby on Rails, Backbone.js and PostgreSQL among other technologies. It was one of the hardest things I've ever done due to the amount of information needed to absorb and retain over such a short period. And it was the best decision I ever made!

I currently reside in San Francisco, California and when I'm not coding, I like to get out and enjoy this amazing city. It's truly impossible to get bored in a place like this and I'm consistently grateful for everything that's led me here.

I'm currently available for full-time, contract, and freelance work. Feel free to hit me up for networking purposes, potential projects, or just to say hi!
